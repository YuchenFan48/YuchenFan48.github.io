# Research Projects

## Untangle the KNOT: Towards Probing and Resolving Knowledge Conflicts in Large Language Models
- **Role:** Co-first author
- **Contributions:**
  - This research delves into the ability of large language models (LLMs) to handle counterfactual knowledge.
  - My contributions include participation in discussions and processing of raw data. Specifically, I employed the PageRank algorithm on Wikidata-5m to identify prevalent entities and relations.

## Explore the Ability of LLMs to Deal with Counter-intuitive Knowledge
- **Contribution:**
  - This project involved a comprehensive survey on how LLMs respond to counter-intuitive knowledge. I discovered a technique named "palter" that can mislead even advanced models like GPT-4.
  - Using text-davinci-003, I expanded an initial set of 80 manually created examples to an extensive dataset of 800 examples.
  - However, quantifying the degree to which LLMs succumb to "palter" proved challenging.

## Evaluating Generative Language Models in Information Extraction as Subjective Question Correction (Short Paper)
- **Role:** Co-first Author, First Rank
- **Contributions:**
  - Integrated requests with `gpt-3.5-turbo` and various tools for comprehensive data acquisition and processing, creating a novel dataset with over 10,000 entries.
  - Fine-tuned `tulu-7b`, `vicuna-7b`, and `llama2-7b` models on multi-GPU setups and evaluated their feasibility on validation sets.
  - Conducted replication and assessment of traditional state-of-the-art (SOTA) Information Extraction (IE) models, including `Text2Event`, `EEQA`, `TPLinker`, and `UIE`.
  - Developed new evaluation metrics to assess model performance.
  - Employed VLLM (Variable Length Language Model) for LLM (Large Language Model)-based inference tasks such as Event Detection (ED), Relation Extraction (RE), and Entity Attribute Extraction (EAE).
